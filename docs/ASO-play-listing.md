## Long description (soru-cevap + senaryo formatı)
```
Waiting for something to happen online? Stop refreshing the page.

Tell Whenly the moment you are waiting for — in one plain sentence — and it
watches public web sources for you, then sends a push notification (or rings
a real alarm) when it happens.

WHAT CAN YOU WATCH?
• Restock alerts — "tell me when the PS5 is back in stock at an official retailer"
• Price drop alerts — "alert me when this flight drops below $250"
• Ticket on-sale alerts — "notify me when this tour goes on sale"
• New listing alerts — "tell me when a 2-bedroom under $1,500 is listed here"
• Tender, grant & regulation alerts for your business
• Any page or topic — "alert me when this page publishes something new"

HOW IS WHENLY DIFFERENT?
• One sentence is the whole setup. No URL hunting, no rule engine, no selectors.
• Compound conditions in plain language: "in stock AND under $500".
• Alarm mode (Pro): critical alerts ring your phone instead of dying in the tray.
• 11 interface languages. Web + Android from the same app.
• Private by default: no ads, no selling your data; export or delete anytime.

HONEST LIMITS (so you know exactly what you get)
Whenly watches public web pages only — it cannot log into portals or pages
behind passwords/captchas, and checks run at regular intervals, not per-second.

FREE TO START
3 active watches, no time limit, no card. Pro adds more watches, more frequent
checks and alarm mode.

Write one sentence. Next time it happens, you know first.
```
